✅ Accepted on Codeforces! Problem A — Marisa Steals Reimu's Takeout | Round 1098 (Div. 2)

Counting with a clever "paired deduction" formula — and a Touhou-themed problem name! 🌸🍱

🔗Problem Link: https://lnkd.in/gbWmWDVY

🧩 The Problem:
 You have items labeled 0, 1, or 2 (representing difficulty levels or types). Marisa can "pair" a 1 and a 2 together for a combined cost. Find the minimum number of "steals" (groups)!
From the code:
c0, c1, c2 = counts of 0s, 1s, 2s
p = min(c1, c2) → pair as many 1s with 2s as possible
ans = c0 + p + (c1-p)//3 + (c2-p)//3
So: zeros contribute individually, paired 1-2s count as 1 group each, unpaired 1s or 2s group in triples! 🎯

💡 The Logic:
Zeros: each costs 1 steal → c0
Paired (1+2): each pair = 1 steal → p = min(c1, c2)
Remaining 1s: (c1-p) leftover 1s, group in 3s → (c1-p)//3
Remaining 2s: (c2-p) leftover 2s, group in 3s → (c2-p)//3
Total = c0 + p + (c1-p)//3 + (c2-p)//3 ✅

Examples:
n=4, w=[0,0,0,0] → c0=4 → 4 ✅
n=3, w=[1,2,0] → p=1, ans=1+1+0+0=2 ✅
n=5, w=[1,2,1,2,1] → c1=3,c2=2,p=2 → 0+2+1//3+0//3=2 ✅

📌 Key Takeaways:
🍱 Greedy pairing first — always pair complementary items before grouping same-type items. Pairs are more efficient than triples! 🎯
🍱 (count-p)//3 for triple grouping — floor division by 3 gives the number of complete triples from leftover items! ✅
🍱 min(c1, c2) for maximum pairing — the bottleneck is always the smaller count! 🔢
