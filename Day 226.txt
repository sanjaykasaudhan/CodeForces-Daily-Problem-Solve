🚀 Codeforces Accepted | Fireworks 🎆💻

Today's problem, "Fireworks," is a simple math-based problem where the goal is to determine the maximum number of fireworks visible at the same time. Instead of simulating every launch, the solution comes from identifying how many launches occur before the visibility duration ends.

🔗Problem Link: https://lnkd.in/g-gddJCG

💡 My Approach
🔹 Step 1: Understand the Launch Schedule
The first launcher fires every a minutes.
The second launcher fires every b minutes.
Every firework remains visible for m minutes after being launched.
To maximize the number of visible fireworks, we count how many launches from each launcher are still visible at a given moment.
🔹 Step 2: Count Visible Fireworks from the First Launcher
Within a visibility window of m minutes, the number of launches from the first launcher is:

m // a + 1

The +1 accounts for the launch occurring at the beginning of the interval.
🔹 Step 3: Count Visible Fireworks from the Second Launcher
Similarly, the second launcher contributes:

m // b + 1

visible fireworks.
🔹 Step 4: Compute the Final Answer
Since both launchers operate independently, the total maximum number of visible fireworks is simply:

print(m // a + m // b + 2)

This directly computes the answer without any simulation.
