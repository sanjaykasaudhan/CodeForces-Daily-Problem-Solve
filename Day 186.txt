Two numbers, one parity check, zero complexity! 🍲🔢 The most delightfully simple problem of the day!

🔗Problem Link: https://lnkd.in/grcRi5uk

🧩 The Problem: Given two integers x and y, output NO if both are odd, YES otherwise!
if x % 2 == 1 and y % 2 == 1:
    print("NO")
else:
    print("YES")
That's it! Two odd numbers → NO. Any other combination → YES! 🎯

💡 The Mathematical Insight:
The only "bad" case is when BOTH x and y are odd. In all other cases (both even, or one of each), the answer is YES!
Why? Koshary (an Egyptian dish!) apparently needs at least one even-numbered ingredient or portion to be balanced. Two odd quantities = impossible to divide equally → NO!
Truth table:
x=odd, y=odd → NO ❌
x=odd, y=even → YES ✅
x=even, y=odd → YES ✅
x=even, y=even → YES ✅
Only 1 out of 4 cases fails! 🍽️

Examples:
x=1, y=1 → both odd → NO ✅
x=1, y=2 → one even → YES ✅
x=4, y=6 → both even → YES ✅
x=5, y=9 → both odd → NO ✅
x=7, y=2 → one even → YES ✅
x=10, y=10 → both even → YES ✅

📌 Key Takeaways:
🍲 Check for the "bad" case, not the "good" case — sometimes it's cleaner to identify what makes something FAIL than what makes it SUCCEED. Here: both odd = fail! 🎯
🍲 x % 2 == 1 → odd check. Alternatively: x & 1 == 1 (bitwise AND with 1 checks the last bit). Both are O(1)! 🔢
🍲 AND vs OR in conditions — "both odd" uses AND (x%2==1 AND y%2==1). "At least one odd" would use OR. Getting these right is crucial for correctness! ⚠️
🍲 else covers 3 cases at once — instead of writing three separate if statements for (odd,even), (even,odd), (even,even), the else clause handles all non-NO cases elegantly! ✅
