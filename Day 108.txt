✅ Accepted on Codeforces! Problem B — Reverse a Permutation | Round 1076 (Div. 3)

Permutations are one of my favourite topics in CP — and this problem had a beautiful suffix-based twist! 🔄

🔗Problem Link: https://lnkd.in/guPFUcTt

🧩 The Problem: You're given a permutation p of length n. In one operation, you pick an index l and reverse the subarray p[l..r], where r is the position of the maximum element in the suffix starting at l. You must apply operations until p becomes [1, 2, 3, ..., n] (sorted). Find the resulting permutation after doing all necessary operations — it's guaranteed a solution always exists!

💡 My Approach — Suffix Maximum + Greedy Simulation:
Step 1 — Build a Suffix Maximum array:
suffix_max[i] = maximum value in p[i..n-1]
Built in O(n) from right to left ✅
Step 2 — Find the leftmost "unsorted" position l:
Walk from left while p[l] == suffix_max[l] (meaning this prefix is already the largest possible — already in place)
Stop at the first l where this breaks
Step 3 — Reverse the subarray p[l..r]:
r = index of suffix_max[l] in p
Reverse p[l:r+1] in-place
Step 4 — Print the result! 🎯

📌 Key Takeaways:
🔍 Suffix Maximum tells you the largest value you could ever bring to position l with one reversal — that's the greedy choice!

🎯 Greedy works because: the leftmost unsorted position must be fixed first, and the only valid reversal brings the suffix maximum to the front of the remaining unsorted segment.

🔄 One operation is always enough — a single well-chosen reversal fixes the entire permutation!

🧠 The Elegant Insight: The suffix maximum array transforms a seemingly complex simulation into a clean "find & fix" strategy. Build once, use once, done. That's the beauty of preprocessing! 🏗️
