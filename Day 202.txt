🔍 Problem Insight
We are given the heights of several towers.
The task is to determine how many distinct integer heights can be achieved after the players perform their moves according to the problem rules.

🧠 Key Observation
Instead of simulating every possible move, let's focus on the smallest and largest tower heights.
Let:
minimum height = min(h)
maximum height = max(h)

Every integer height between these two values (inclusive) can be obtained.
Therefore, the answer is simply:
max(h)−min(h)+1\text{max}(h) - \text{min}(h) + 1max(h)−min(h)+1Example
Heights = [2, 6, 4]

min = 2
max = 6

Possible heights:
2, 3, 4, 5, 6

Count =
6 - 2 + 1 = 5

✅ Answer = 5

⚡ Approach
1️⃣ Find the minimum tower height.
2️⃣ Find the maximum tower height.
3️⃣ Count all integers in the inclusive range:

print(max(h) - min(h) + 1)

📈 Complexity
Time Complexity: O(n)
Space Complexity: O(1)
