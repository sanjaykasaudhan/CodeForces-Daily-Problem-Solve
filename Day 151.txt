🧩 The Problem: You have n grains of rice, each weighing between a-b and a+b grams (±b tolerance). The total weight is between c-d and c+d. Can these two constraints be simultaneously satisfied? Answer Yes or No!
The total weight of n grains ranges from n*(a-b) to n*(a+b). The acceptable total is [c-d, c+d]. Do these two intervals overlap? 🎯

💡 The Key Insight — Interval Overlap Check:
Two intervals [lo, hi] and [c-d, c+d] overlap if and only if:
lo ≤ c+d AND hi ≥ c-d
Where:
lo = n * (a - b) (minimum possible total weight)
hi = n * (a + b) (maximum possible total weight)
If these intervals share any point → Yes, the constraints can be satisfied! ✅

📌 Key Takeaways:
🍚 Two intervals overlap iff lo ≤ hi2 AND hi ≥ lo2 — this is the universal interval overlap formula. Memorise it: [a,b] ∩ [c,d] ≠ ∅ ⟺ a≤d AND b≥c! 🎯
🍚 Scale individual bounds to get total bounds — when n independent items each have range [a-b, a+b], the total range is [n*(a-b), n*(a+b)]. Direct scaling, no summation needed! ✅
🍚 No simulation needed — don't iterate over possible total weights. Just compute the interval bounds and check overlap in O(1)! ⚡
🍚 Watch for negative lower bounds — if a < b, then a-b < 0. Handle this gracefully (Python integers handle it fine, but be careful in other languages)! ⚠️
