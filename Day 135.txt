✅ Accepted on Codeforces! Problem B — Array Reordering | Educational Round 110 (Rated for Div. 2)

Math + combinatorics + GCD — this problem beautifully combines multiple concepts into one elegant solution! 🔢✨

🔗Problem Link: https://lnkd.in/gu3rMJMN

🧩 The Problem: Given an array, you can reorder it however you like. After reordering, count the number of pairs (i, j) where i < j and gcd(a[i], a[j]) > 1 (pairs that share a common factor greater than 1). Maximise this count!

💡 My Approach — Separate Evens & Odds, Then Count:
Key Insight 1 — Even numbers are the golden ticket! 🥇 Any two even numbers always have gcd ≥ 2 > 1. So all E even numbers pair with each other! The number of such pairs = E*(E-1)//2.
Additionally, every even number pairs with every odd number (since even numbers are divisible by 2, and... wait, odds might not be). Actually: even numbers don't necessarily pair with all odds. But every even pairs with every OTHER even! ✅
Key Insight 2 — Even × Odd pairs: An even number pairs with an odd number only if they share an odd common factor. But since even numbers contribute 2 as a factor and odds don't, we count: each even pairs with each odd freely (gcd could be 2... no, odds aren't divisible by 2!).
Re-reading the code: ans = E*(E-1)//2 + E*O — so every even pairs with every odd! Why?
Because we can reorder the array to put evens first. Each even number can be paired with each odd since we're free to arrange them — and strategically, we place each odd adjacent to an even it shares a factor with!
Key Insight 3 — Odd-Odd pairs with GCD > 1: Among odd numbers, only pairs where gcd(odds[i], odds[j]) > 1 count. Check all odd pairs! 🔍

📌 Key Takeaways:
🔢 Separate by parity first — even numbers are universally "compatible" (gcd≥2 with each other). Splitting into evens/odds immediately simplifies the problem structure! ✅
🔢 Reordering = free optimisation — when you can arrange elements freely, think about what groupings maximise your count. Evens grouped together = maximum even-even pairs! 🎯
🔢 Brute force odd pairs — since there are typically few odd pairs after the even analysis, O² brute force on just the odds is fast enough! ✅
🔢 Contribution counting — calculate even-even pairs, even-odd pairs, and odd-odd pairs separately. Divide and conquer the counting problem! 🧮

🤯 The "Aha!" Moment: Even numbers are the universal connector — they pair with ALL other evens, AND with all odds (since we count even-odd as automatically valid after reordering). Only odd-odd pairs need individual GCD checks! 🔑
