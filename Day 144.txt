🧩 The Problem: You're given a string containing only - (debt) and _ (gold) characters. You need to form "valid pairs" — count the number of ways to arrange them into valid goblin accounting! 🪙
Reading the code: count - signs (D) and _ underscores (U). Then:
Split D into two groups: d1 = D // 2 and d2 = D - d1
Answer = d1 * d2 * U
So the problem asks: how many ways can you pick one - from the first half, one - from the second half, and one _? It's a counting problem! 🎯

💡 The Mathematical Insight:
The dashes - can be split into two groups:
d1 = D // 2 (floor half)
d2 = D - d1 = D - D//2 (ceiling half)
Each valid triple requires:
One dash from group 1 → d1 choices
One dash from group 2 → d2 choices
One underscore → U choices
By the multiplication principle: answer = d1 × d2 × U
Why split D into floor and ceiling halves? Because D dashes can be paired up — d1 dashes go "before" and d2 go "after" in valid accounting pairs, and each combination with an underscore forms a valid entry! 🏦

📌 Key Takeaways:
🪙 count() for character frequency — Python's str.count() is the go-to for counting specific characters in O(n). Clean and readable! 🐍
🪙 Floor + ceiling split — splitting D into D//2 and D - D//2 is a clean way to divide items into two nearly-equal groups. This handles both even and odd D gracefully! ✅
🪙 Multiplication principle — independent choices multiply. d1 × d2 × U = (ways to pick from group 1) × (ways to pick from group 2) × (ways to pick underscore). Classic combinatorics! 🎲
