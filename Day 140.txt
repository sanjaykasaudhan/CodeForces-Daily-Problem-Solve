✅ Accepted on Codeforces! Problem A — Everyone Loves to Sleep | Round 811 (Div. 3)

Time arithmetic with a real-world flavour — and a beautifully clean modular solution! ⏰😴

🔗Problem Link: https://lnkd.in/gz9qBF-2

🧩 The Problem: You go to bed at time H:M (hours:minutes). You have n alarms set at various times. Find the minimum waiting time until the next alarm goes off — i.e., which alarm rings soonest after your bedtime? Output the wait in hours and minutes!
The clock is 24-hour circular — so times wrap around midnight! 🔄

💡 My Approach — Convert to Minutes + Circular Distance:
Step 1 — Convert bedtime to total minutes:
bed = H * 60 + M
Step 2 — For each alarm, compute circular wait time:
alarm = h * 60 + m
wait = (alarm - bed) % (24 * 60)
The % (24 * 60) handles the wrap-around beautifully:
If alarm is after bed → positive wait ✅
If alarm is before bed (next day) → wraps around via modulo ✅
If alarm equals bed → wait = 0 ✅ (already time to wake up!)
Step 3 — Track minimum wait across all alarms:
min_wait = min(min_wait, wait)
Step 4 — Convert back to hours and minutes:
print(min_wait // 60, min_wait % 60)

Examples:
Bed=6:13, alarms: [8:0, 12:30, 14:45, 6:0] → waits: [107, 377, 511, 1427] → min=107 → 1 47 ✅
Bed=3:60... (12:30=750min) wait= (360-390)%1440=1410 → min=0 → 0 0 ✅
Bed=23:35, alarms: [20:15, 10:30] → waits: [(1215-1415)%1440=1240, (630-1415)%1440=655] → min=655 → 10 55 ✅

📌 Key Takeaways:
⏰ Always convert time to a single unit — working in minutes (not hours+minutes separately) eliminates all carry/borrow issues. One number, zero edge cases! ✅
⏰ Modulo is the circular clock operator — (alarm - bed) % (24*60) handles ALL cases: same day, next day, exact match. This single formula replaces complex if-else chains! 🎯
⏰ float('inf') initialisation — perfect sentinel for minimum tracking when you don't know the range of values. Always use it for clean min-tracking loops! 🐍
⏰ Convert back at the end — // 60 for hours, % 60 for minutes. The inverse of the initial conversion. Keep this pair as a mental template! 🧮

🤯 The "Aha!" Moment: (alarm - bed) % (24 * 60) is the entire key. A negative difference (alarm before bed) wraps around via modulo to give the correct "next day" wait time. One formula handles a 24-hour circular clock perfectly! 🔑
