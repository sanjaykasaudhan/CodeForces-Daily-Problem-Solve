🚀 Codeforces Accepted | Painting the Ribbon 🎨💻

Today's challenge, "Painting the Ribbon," was a great example of how a simple mathematical observation can replace complex simulation. Instead of checking every possible repainting scenario, the solution comes from analyzing the maximum frequency of any color after Alice paints the ribbon.

🔗Problem Link: https://lnkd.in/gVCuwqRf

💡 My Approach
🔹 Step 1: Distribute the Ribbon Optimally
Alice wants to make it as difficult as possible for Bob to repaint the ribbon into a single color.
The best strategy is to distribute the n ribbon parts as evenly as possible among the m colors.
The maximum number of parts that any one color can receive is:
maxFrequency=⌈nm⌉\text{maxFrequency} = \left\lceil \frac{n}{m} \right\rceilmaxFrequency=⌈mn​⌉which can be computed efficiently as:

maxFrequency = (n + m - 1) // m

🔹 Step 2: Analyze Bob's Repainting
Bob can repaint at most k ribbon parts.
After Alice's optimal coloring:
The largest color group already has maxFrequency parts.
The remaining parts are:
n−maxFrequencyn - \text{maxFrequency}n−maxFrequencyIf Bob can repaint all of these remaining parts (k ≥ n - maxFrequency), then every ribbon part can become the same color.
Otherwise, at least one part will always remain different.
🔹 Step 3: Final Decision
If
n - maxFrequency > k

➡️ Print "YES"
(Alice can prevent Bob from making the ribbon a single color.)
Otherwise,
➡️ Print "NO"
(Bob can repaint enough parts to make the ribbon monochromatic.)

⚙️ Complexity Analysis
Time Complexity: O(1) per test case
Space Complexity: O(1)
A constant-time solution achieved purely through mathematical reasoning.
